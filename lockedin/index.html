You know the feeling

The first half
quietly got away.

You set something in motion back in January. By now you're not quite sure where it went.

The alarm you used to beat. The sessions that slowly became "tomorrow." The plan that slipped off the calendar without a decision ever being made.

Nobody plans to drift. And the back half is about to go the exact same way, unless something changes it.

Locked In isn't a challenge. It's a line in the sand. Six weeks to build the one habit that carries you all the way to December.

The foundation is the same for everyone: keep your sessions ticking over, and lock in one focus of your own. If you're already training four or five times a week, the sessions are second nature, so this is about that one thing. If you're just finding your rhythm, getting movement consistent is the win. Both count. Both start here.

2Personal · you sign

Your one thing

Two things carry you through the six weeks: your sessions ticking over, and one focus you choose. If you already train four or five times a week, the sessions are second nature, so this is all about that one thing. If you're still building the habit, getting consistent is the win, and your one thing keeps you honest. Not ten habits. One. Bed before nine. A litre of water a day. Ten pages. Something small enough to keep every day and real enough to change something by the end. You write it, you sign it, and it's yours to hold.

  • One habit, chosen by you, kept private
